Hello builders and makers,

In this update, I am finally saying goodbye to the EV3, its motors, its sensors
and all the memories I have of building with it. But I have prepared a proper
farewell.

## Documentation overhaul

﻿I have now fully documented both
[the overhauled EV3
code](https://codeberg.org/peterb/sort_bot-control) and [the new Raspberry Pi
code](https://codeberg.org/peterb/sort_bot). If you have a little bit of experience with EV3 and
Raspberry Pi, it should take less than an hour to get all the software up and
running.

And that is only going to improve.

Compared to the original garbled mess of Python code, the current software is
much more reliable. It is a lot easier to set up and does not require hours to
train and finetune a deep learning network.

While it is not yet perfect, I think it is close to what it should have been
from the start. Even though I am replacing the EV3, the Raspberry Pi software
is going to continue to receive improvements.

If you can't tell. I am proud and happy with the current state of the
machine.

## New beginnings

But I am not resting. I have started the first experiments with the Raspberry
Pi Build HAT and Powered Up motors.

The goal is to make the machine more accessible. The Powered Up motors are much
easier to obtain. You may already have them as they are used in quite a few
motorized Lego sets.

Together with the move to Powered Up, I have a number of other improvements and
changes I want to implement. I have already talked about some of my ideas in
previous newsletters, but now I am actually going to build and test them.

More on that next month.

As always, your feedback is absolutely welcome. Thanks for following along on
this journey. Happy sorting, and I look forward to sharing more updates soon!
